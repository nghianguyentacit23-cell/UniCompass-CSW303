## Example Story Card

Title: Plan next semester courses

User story: As a student preparing for course registration, I want to see suitable course suggestions so that I can register with more confidence.

Acceptance criteria:

1. Given I have completed some courses, when I open semester planning, then I see a list of suitable course options.
2. Given a course may not be suitable for me, when I select it, then I see a clear warning and a suggested alternative.

Tasks:

1. Draft the screen flow.
2. Define the required student information.
3. Prepare sample course data.
4. Create a simple prototype or mock screen.

Definition of Done:

1. Card has owner and priority.
2. Acceptance criteria are written.
3. Task is placed in the correct board list.

## Week 2 Interview Plan

Purpose: Validate student pain points related to course registration, academic planning, GPA targets, and study progress risks.

Target interviewees:

| No. | Target Participant | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | First-year student | Understand confusion during early course planning. |
| 2 | Second-year student | Understand transition from basic courses to major courses. |
| 3 | Third-year student | Understand concerns about prerequisites, internship preparation, and GPA. |
| 4 | Student with experience retaking a course | Understand recovery planning after a failed course. |
| 5 | Academic advisor or senior student mentor | Understand common student mistakes and recurring questions. |
| 6 | Career-oriented student | Understand planning around internships, scholarships, and graduation goals. |

Consent line:

Before we start, we would like to ask for your permission to use your anonymous feedback for our CSW303 course project. We will not collect sensitive personal information, and your answers will only be used to improve our project requirements.

Interview questions:

1. How do you usually decide which courses to register for each semester?
2. What information is hardest to understand during course registration?
3. Have you ever worried that one course could affect later semesters? What happened?
4. How do you currently check whether your GPA goal is still achievable?
5. What tools do you use now for academic planning?
6. What kind of warning or suggestion would help you make better academic decisions?
7. If an app gave you course planning advice, what would make you trust it?
8. What is one academic planning problem you wish the university system solved better?
